How is the Balloon Placed?

No surgery. No anesthesia. No endoscopy.

01

Swallow the Capsule

You swallow a small capsule containing the deflated balloon, attached to a thin tube.

02

X-Ray Confirmation

A quick X-ray confirms the capsule has reached your stomach correctly.

03

Fill the Balloon

The doctor fills the balloon with 550ml of liquid through the thin tube.

04

Verify Placement

A second X-ray confirms the balloon is properly filled and positioned.

05

All Done

The tube is gently removed. The entire process takes about 15 minutes.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the Allurion Gastric Balloon program

The Allurion Balloon is placed without surgery or endoscopy. You swallow a capsule containing the deflated balloon, which is attached to a thin tube. Once confirmed in position via X-ray, the doctor fills the balloon with liquid through the tube, then gently removes the tube. The entire procedure takes about 15 minutes.

The procedure itself is not painful. Most patients experience some discomfort or nausea in the first few days as their body adjusts to the balloon. Our medical team provides guidance and support to help you through this adjustment period.

After approximately 4 months, the balloon naturally deflates and passes through your digestive system without any intervention or removal procedure required.

Most patients return to normal activities within 1-2 days. We recommend planning for at least 1-2 days of rest after the procedure to allow your body to adjust to the balloon.
